Relativistic Cars

This demonstrates the effects on an observer's perception of the cars of relativistic speeds. If the cars were to travel at speeds close to that of the speed of light, length contraction would result in the cars appearing to have different lengths to if they were at rest. This could potentially alter the relative observed lengths of the cars enough that the shorter car were never fully obscured.
The animation runs three times, each with the blue car travelling at a different speed. The speeds are written at the bottom-left; v1 is the speed of the red car, and v2 that of the blue. The rest length of the blue car remains constant between animations (as does that of the red); the only difference is the length contraction caused by the speed relative to an observer.
Unlike in other animations, this animation shows the linear, rather than rotational, speeds of the cars.

 
 
    • These animations are still being refined for access over the internet. They are most reliable in Safari, lag in Edge and Opera and are poor in Chrome, while other browsers are untested.
    • Animations were produced in Processing, and uploaded using Processing.js. They can be restarted by refreshing the page, or, once finished, by clicking the animation canvas.
