Interpolation

In this animation, a stationary obstruction to the view of the observer has been added. The blue line on the graph, drawn when the car is fully obscured, represents the rotational velocity at which an observer would likely estimate the car to be moving due to incorrect interpolation. The difference between the estimated rotational velocity, in blue, and the true rotational velocity, in red, therefore demonstrates the difference an obstruction can make to an observer's understanding of the motion of an object.

 
 
    • These animations are still being refined for access over the internet. They are most reliable in Safari, lag in Edge and Opera and are poor in Chrome, while other browsers are untested.
    • Animations were produced in Processing, and uploaded using Processing.js. They can be restarted by refreshing the page, or, once finished, by clicking the animation canvas.
